I passed the AWS Certified Generative AI Developer – Professional. And because I took it as a Beta exam — before AWS had published pass rates or calibrated the difficulty publicly — I also received the Early Adopter badge. Two badges. One decision: go early, no safety net.

🏅 [AWS Certified Generative AI Developer – Professional](https://www.credly.com/badges/7446afbf-7fa1-4450-be74-7e34e5454192)
🏅 [AWS Early Adopter](https://www.credly.com/earner/earned/badge/c4bf319f-af9f-468a-a8c3-127712f2e2f1)

But the moment I keep thinking about didn't happen when the results came through. It happened at question 67.

It was a Beta exam — 85 questions instead of 75, no score at the end, no public pass rates to calibrate against. Somewhere in the middle of a complex orchestration scenario, my brain stopped trying to answer the question and started designing the system. I caught myself, selected the answer, and kept going.

That involuntary drift was the clearest signal I could have gotten: the way I prepared had worked. Not because I'd memorized the right content, but because I'd spent months building production systems on these services before I ever opened a study guide.

Here's what the tutorials don't tell you about Professional-level AWS exams: they don't test recall. They test judgment — the ability to reason about trade-offs you've never seen before, in architectures you're encountering for the first time, under time pressure. That kind of judgment doesn't come from reading. It comes from building.

The resources that made the difference weren't secret. AWS Skill Builder gave me conceptual depth. Two Udemy courses (practice exams + conceptual coverage) gave me scenario fluency. But the real differentiator was the lab I'd built months earlier — an insurance claim processing pipeline on Amazon Bedrock — and the ADRs I wrote every time I made an architecture decision I wasn't sure about.

The exam scenarios felt like a project backlog. That's how you know you're ready.

If you're preparing for this certification: build something real before you study. Document the decisions. The exam will feel like home.
